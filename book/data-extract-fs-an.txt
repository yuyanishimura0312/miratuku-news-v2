A.1 TIME-RELATED CONCEPTS (TEMPORALITY, FUTURE, PAST)
================================================================================
These concepts directly engage with temporal thinking and futures orientation:

1. FUTURE-FOCUSED ANTHROPOLOGY CONCEPTS
   - c483: Anthropology of Foresight (フォーサイトの人類学)
     Definition: Critiques how foresight practices construct futures and whose futures
     are included/excluded. Bridges foresight and anthropology.
     CLA Affinity: systemic, worldview

   - c484: Anthropology of Scenarios (シナリオの人類学)
     Definition: Analyzes scenario planning as performative practice reflecting
     organizational cognition and power relations. Studies narrative structure and
     organizational effects.
     CLA Affinity: systemic, worldview

   - c152: Ethnography of the Future (未来の民族誌)
     Definition: Ethnographic methodology for studying how people construct and
     practice futures. Encompasses imaginative, emotional dimensions of future-thinking.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

   - c151: Anthropology of Anticipation (予期の人類学)
     Definition: Analyzes how people predict/anticipate futures and how this shapes
     present action. Studies risk management, insurance, investment as future-oriented practices.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

   - c453: Speculative Ethnography (未来民族誌)
     Definition: Methodological approach to imagine possible futures using ethnographic
     knowledge. Integrates design thinking and foresight methods.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

2. HOPE & POSSIBILITY CONCEPTS
   - c153: Anthropology of Hope (希望の人類学)
     Definition: Analyzes how people construct and maintain hope in difficult situations.
     Captures active future-orientation and pursuit of possibility beyond mere optimism.
     CLA Affinity: myth

   - c497: Anthropology as Hope (希望としての人類学)
     Definition: Anthropology as source of hope through demonstrating alternative
     possible worlds. Emphasizes "difficult hope" rooted in justice awareness.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

   - c499: Indigenous Futurism (先住民未来主義)
     Definition: Artistic/intellectual movement using indigenous cosmologies and
     aesthetics to construct futures. Decolonial source for future imagination.
     CLA Affinity: myth, worldview

3. TEMPORALITY & TIME CONCEPTS
   - c156: Temporality (テンポラリティ)
     Definition: Framework analyzing how time is socially/culturally constructed
     as multilayered experience. Studies coexistence/conflict of multiple temporalities.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

   - c194: Ethnographic Present (民族誌的現在)
     Definition: Critique of anthropology's tendency to write about societies as
     fixed/timeless. Fabian's "denial of coevalness" - erasing target society's
     historicity and change potential. Central to postcolonial anthropology.
     CLA Affinity: systemic

   - c155: Waiting (待つこと)
     Definition: Anthropology of the act of waiting in bureaucracy, immigration,
     healthcare. Waiting embodies power asymmetry, erodes agency, temporalizes uncertainty.
     CLA Affinity: litany

4. MYTHIC/COSMOLOGICAL TIME CONCEPTS
   - c428: Dreamtime (アボリジニのドリームタイム)
     Definition: Aboriginal Australian concept where ancestral beings' creative time
     is not past but ontologically present through land, ritual, law. Questions Western
     time/space/subjectivity concepts fundamentally.
     CLA Affinity: myth, worldview

   - c181: Ma/In-between Space-Time (間)
     Definition: Japanese concept of meaningful void between entities. Dynamic
     relational space-time generated by context. Contrasts with Western object-based
     spatial thinking.
     CLA Affinity: worldview

   - c048: Taskscape (タスクスケープ)
     Definition: Ingold's concept of landscape as temporal pattern of practical
     activities. Environment as "lived" through rhythmic activity, not just "seen"
     as static visual space.
     CLA Affinity: myth, systemic

5. PAST-ORIENTED CONCEPTS
   - c451: Archaeo-Ethnography (考古民族誌)
     Definition: Integrates archaeological and ethnographic evidence to analyze past
     societies. Studies material culture persistence, technology transmission, historical
     landscape construction.
     CLA Affinity: systemic, worldview

A.3 INDIGENOUS KNOWLEDGE & TRADITION CONCEPTS
================================================================================

1. Indigenous Methodologies (先住民の方法論)
   Tuhiwai Smith (1999) - Critiques research methodology's coloniality. Demands
   community self-determination, knowledge as commons, story/oral/ritual/dream
   as valid knowledge forms.

2. Local/Indigenous Knowledge (ローカル知)
   Place-rooted, intergenerationally transmitted practical knowledge. Revalued
   in development/environment/health. Critical knowledge politics issue.

3. Oral Tradition (口承伝承)
   Vansina's methodology for treating oral transmission as historical evidence.
   Studies formal/informal transmission, mechanisms of change. Relativizes
   text-centric historiography.

4. Indigenous Futurism (先住民未来主義)
   *Already listed above - Arts/intellectual movement using indigenous worldviews
   for future imagination

5. Moral Economy (モラルエコノミー)
   *Already listed above - Pre-market ethical norms governing economic behavior

CONCEPTUAL BRIDGE: ANTHROPOLOGY → FUTURES
================================================================================

Key theoretical bridges for book "The Future Lies Behind Us":

1. TEMPORALITY AS CULTURAL CONSTRUCT
   Anthropology recognizes that "time" is not universal:
   - Cyclical time (Dreamtime, seasonal patterns)
   - Layered time (mythic + historical + present)
   - Relational time (Ma, Taskscape)
   - Embedded time (Waiting, Embodiment)

   Futures implication: Western linear progress model is culturally specific,
   not universal. Future-thinking varies ontologically.

2. PAST ≠ FIXED / FUTURE ≠ OPEN
   Ethnographic Present critique shows how anthropologists themselves construct
   "timeless" pasts. Indigenous Futurism shows how past (cosmology, myth) actively
   generates futures.

   Futures implication: Past is "alive" in cultural ontologies. Future is
   constructed through past-as-present.

3. HOPE AS ANTHROPOLOGICAL CATEGORY
   "Anthropology of Hope" and "Indigenous Futurism" provide non-technocratic
   frameworks for understanding how communities construct viable futures amid
   difficulty. Not prediction, but possibility-making.

   Futures implication: Futures work is ethical/political, not merely analytical.
   Indigenous methodologies offer epistemological alternatives to Western foresight.

4. ONTOLOGICAL PLURALITY
   Perspectivism, Animism, Cyborg, Multispecies Ethnography recognize multiple
   coexisting realities/temporalities. Not "perspectives on one world" but
   genuinely different worlds.

   Futures implication: Future-thinking must account for multiple simultaneous
   worldviews, not just scenario plurality.

5. MYTHIC/WORLDVIEW LAYER (CLA)
   The concentration of concepts in myth/worldview affinity suggests anthropology's
   most generative insights occur at ontological/cosmological level, not systemic
   surface.

   Futures implication: Futures work that ignores mythic/worldview layer risks
   shallow prediction. Deep futures work must engage cosmological imagination.
